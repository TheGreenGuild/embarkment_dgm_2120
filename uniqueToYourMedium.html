Mediums

Each medium has unique strengths and weaknesses associated with them. Learning to take advantage of your medium's special strengths and how to avoid it's weaknesses can be one of your most powerful tools in storytelling.
In film for example, you can easily show a silent expression exchanged without dialigue by two characters and imply what they are thinking. Typically you cannot hear what they are thinking though. It is a-typical to have a voice over of their thoughts, but in a book hearing a characters thoughts will not draw a second glance from the audience.

Writing

Writing is awesome because it's easier than other mediums to choose a viewpoint style and narrative closeness. A fantastic resource is Brandon Sanderson's YouTube page where he has posted many of his lectures from a course he teaches at BYU University. His lectures are longer (hour plus) and each one ranges in topics. Ellen Brock a freelance editor on YouTube also has a great page with smaller bite sized videos on specific topics if you're looking for something more specific.

Strengths

  • Total freedom
  • No budget constraint. Props and set are all made by the reader. You can literally make anything happen. No CGI needed.

Weaknesses

  1. The reader is always reading words so it's harder to use "silent moments" between characters like movies can do.
  2. Harder to use color for thematic elements than visual mediums.
  3. No use of music.
  4. Easier to disregard the importance of getting other's input. With film for example you naturally get input as part of a team.

Film

Film is amazing beacuse you can show not tell which is an old addage in story telling. You can lean on (or be hindered by) an actor's performance as well.

Silent moments
Dialogue can stop for a moment and purely visual story telling can take over for a moment.
Parallel Action
Cutting back and forth quickly between two scenes simultaneously is much easier to accomplish in this medium than in written form.
Visual Cues
Certain visual cues such as color, facial expressions, and high/low camera angles can convey meaning in ways that pure dialouge or description may not be able to convey as easily or at all.

Strengths

  • Use of visual story telling elements such as color music etc.
  • Use of acting, lighting, music and other elements to reinforce a script.

Weaknesses

  • Can be more time consuming.
  • Lots more budget, skills or resources to pull off correctly.

Webtoon

A quickly growing medium with low barrier to entry on the website. Anyone can submit their work for free and use Webtoon's tools to enhance their page.

Strengths

  • Keeping digital copies of your work means you have the potential to convert it to a physical graphic novel later.
  • Possible to have fans contribute via GoFundMe links.

Weaknesses

  • Not selling anything directly.
  • Audience can only grow so big using this one online platform.
